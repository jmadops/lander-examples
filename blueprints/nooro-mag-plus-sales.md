## Copy Patterns

- **Headline formula:** Attention Stat + Problem + Product Solution — "ATTENTION: 82% Of Women Suffer From Imbalanced Hormones & Low Energy" → "Here's Why [Product] Is The Secret to [Benefit]"
- **Body copy structure:** Founder-narrative-driven (Rachel's story). Follows the "I tried everything → discovered the missing link → here's what it is" story arc. Short paragraphs, punchy sentences. Heavy use of bold and italic for emphasis. Conversational, empathetic, female-to-female tone. Progressive disclosure: story → science → proof → offer.
- **Claims presentation:** "X% of women" stat for attention. "4% absorption rate" for competitor weakness. "300+ functions" for ingredient authority. "90% less magnesium" for modern farming critique. Comparison table with checks vs X marks. Strikethrough pricing for value stacking. Asterisks (*) for disclaimer compliance.
- **Social proof patterns:** "20,519 reviews" aggregate count, 5 named carousel testimonials with photos, Instagram-style UGC review cards (8 images), founder personal story, "Clinically Studied" badge, medical white paper with academic citations
- **Urgency/scarcity tactics:** Countdown timer (days/hours/min/sec), "GET 70% OFF" limited offer, "February has a very high risk of selling out ⚠️", same-day shipping countdown, "restocks take 6-8 weeks", sticky bottom bar "ENDS SOON", special offer popup
- **Emoji usage patterns:** ⚠️ for scarcity warnings. Emoji icons for timeline milestones (🛌😌⚡️🌺🧘‍♀️🔥). Minimal in body copy — used structurally for visual scanning.
