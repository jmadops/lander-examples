## Copy Patterns

- **Headline formula:** Authority + Conspiracy Reveal + Dollar Amount — "Top Vascular Specialist Exposes the $18 Billion Secret the Swollen Legs Industry Doesn't Want You to Know..."
- **Body copy structure:** Extremely long-form (5,000+ words), first-person narrative from a doctor character ("Dr. Mark Evans"), conspiracy/exposé tone. Short punchy paragraphs. Heavy use of em-dashes. Rhetorical questions. One-word sentences for impact ("Useless." "Gone." "Fraud.").
- **Claims presentation:** Bold for key statements, ALL CAPS for emphasis, italicized for journal citations, specific numbers throughout (dollar amounts, percentages, dates, times). Medical terminology used authoritatively.
- **Social proof patterns:** Hyper-specific testimonials (full name, age, occupation, city), fake comment section with threaded replies, statistical results (12,847+ users, 91% success rate, 0.9% return rate), competitor product comparisons by name/price
- **Urgency/scarcity tactics:** 72-hour countdown, unit inventory count (2,847 remaining), production capacity limits (400/week), previous sellout story (14 hours), real-time sales velocity (67/hour), "page expires" warning, legal threats as urgency narrative
- **Emoji usage patterns:** ⚠️ used once in H2 for urgency. 👁 used as view counter. Minimal emoji overall — medical/authoritative tone.
