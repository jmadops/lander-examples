## Copy Patterns

- **Headline formula:** Statistic + Fear + Promise — "Why 80% of Parkinson's Patients Never Fully Recover From Freezing. (And How This Device Fixed What $23,000 in Treatments Couldn't)"
- **Body copy structure:** Long-form editorial (3,000+ words), written from the perspective of a "movement disorder specialist," conversational but authoritative tone. Paragraphs are short (2-4 sentences). Heavy use of em-dashes for dramatic pauses.
- **Claims presentation:** Key claims are bolded inline. Critical statements use yellow/orange background highlights. Dollar amounts are always bolded. Statistics are bolded. Medical terms are italicized.
- **Social proof patterns:** Expert doctor endorsements with photos and credentials, extended patient case study (Harold), Facebook-style comment thread with multiple users, star ratings with specific numbers (4.8/5, 3,791 ratings), specific patient count ("over 900 patients")
- **Urgency/scarcity tactics:** "Parkinson's is progressive" repeated throughout, "before Parkinson's takes more from you" repeated, inventory scarcity notice ("flying off the shelves"), time-limited offer ("Ends Sunday"), progressive deterioration framing, "your next fall gets closer"
- **Emoji usage patterns:** Checkmarks (✓) used in the comparison box benefits list. No other emojis used. Clean, medical/editorial tone throughout.

  - Three separate expert endorsements with photos add clinical legitimacy
