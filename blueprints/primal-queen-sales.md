## Copy Patterns

- **Headline formula:** Problem/Benefit + Ancestral Authority — "Here's Why Beef Organ Superfoods Are The Secret to Healthy Hormones & Energy"
- **Body copy structure:** Narrative-driven, founder-story format. Alternates between emotional storytelling (Shelby's journey), scientific credibility (nutrient comparisons, organ benefits), and girlfriend-chat tone ("are we queens?"). Short paragraphs, punchy sentences. Heavy use of bold for emphasis. Conversational and empowering.
- **Claims presentation:** "X MORE [nutrient] than [vegetable]" comparison format for each organ. Bold for key stats. Timeline format for results expectations with specific timeframes (1 week to 12 months). Asterisks (*) for disclaimer compliance on health claims.
- **Social proof patterns:** "21,455 Reviews" with 4.6 star average, rotating testimonial carousel in hero (5 named reviewers), Trustpilot-style review section with dates/locations, "4,014 purchases made today" counter, founder personal story as social proof
- **Urgency/scarcity tactics:** Countdown timer (days/hours/min/sec), scrolling marquee "GET 1 MONTH FREE", "February has a very high risk of selling out ⚠️", same-day shipping countdown, "purchases made today" counter, sticky bottom bar "ENDS SOON"
- **Emoji usage patterns:** ⚠️ for scarcity warnings. Emoji icons for timeline milestones (🛌😌⚡️🌺🧘‍♀️🔥). Minimal in body copy — used structurally rather than decoratively.
